Coaches for the 2013 Symposium

The 2013 Atlantic Paddle Symposium Commitee is hard at work finalizing a list of skilled and enthusiastic coaches for the 2013 Symposium.

Check here regularly to watch this list grow!

John Carmody - Special Guest Instructor - Sea Kayak coach

With the exception of a few misguided years a while ago, John has lived on the ocean his entire life. He has been in and around most every type of boat, tending towards the wind and human powered types.

Almost 20 years ago, the sea kayak became his vessel of choice, and he has enjoyed adapting his “Big Boat” skills to kayaks. John’s love of the traditions of the sea is infectious as he passes his seamanship and navigation skills on to his students.

As a Coach, his understanding of the human body and biomechanics gives him unique insights into what will make his students more efficient, safer from injuries and better able to enjoy these centuries old craft in more exposed environments. John has led trips throughout Maine, the Canadian Maritimes, and the British Isles. He regularly instructs at Kayak Symposiums throughout the US.

Joey Hitchins (Ottawa River) White Water Coach

Joey is a family man, professional kayaker, web developer, official CKC representative for Freestyle Kayaking in Ontario, Canada, and two time and current member of the Canadian National Freestyle Kayak Team. He and his fiance Lisa own and operate Liquid Skills, a kayak and stand up paddle boarding school based his beloved Ottawa River.

He is currently training on that infamous whitewater in a new carbon fiber boat of his own design. We're excited about having him on the Coaching Team this year.

Serge Savard (Quebec) Sea Kayak Coach

Serge is all about activity and staying fit, healthy and safe in great surroundings. Serge love the feeling of being in harmony with the water, sitting in his kayak, alert and ready to enjoy the challenge ahead. He makes time to share his considerable experience to help other paddlers become self sufficient.

Born on the Saguenay river shore, moving first to the St Lawrence Gulf and then to the sea, all to discover all the potential to be connected with adventure. He instructs many outdoor skills to individuals, schools, colleges and corporations. He races, paddles and explores.

Mark Scriver - (Ottawa, ON) White Water Canoe and SUP Coach

Mark began his paddling career at summer camps in Ontario. He taught at Kandalore Outdoor centre and moved on to Black Feather Wilderness Adventures in the mid 80's where he continues to guide a trip a year in the Canadian Arctic or Greenland. He has also worked in the paddle sport industry as a retail buyer, sales representative and canoe designer and currently represents many leading paddle sport brands.

He has authored and co-authored several books including, Thrill of the Paddle, Canoe Tripping; an Essential Guide and the Black Feather Camp Cookbook. He competed in white water freestyle for over a decade culminating in hosting the world championships and winning a gold medal. Though best know as a white water canoeist, he enjoys paddling all crafts and SUP is his latest interest.

Andrew Westwood - (Ottawa, ON) White Water Canoe Coach

Andrew Westwood is a wilderness canoe adventurer, whitewater paddler, open canoe slalom competitor and school teacher, living in Ottawa, Ontario, Canada. He has 20 plus years experience instructed canoeing, and coached canoe slalom in both Canada and the United States. Working as a guest instructor for a variety of host organizations, Andrew can frequently be found instructing on one of his favourite rivers at the Madawaska Kanu Centre.

Author of Canoeing, Essential Skills and Safety and DVD titled Canoeing, Andrew is also a regular contributor to Rapidmedia. Always willing to share his instructional experiences, he currently is a member of Paddle Canada’s Canoe Program Development Committee.

Carole Westwood - (Ottawa, ON) White Water Canoe Coach

Carole has been an avid paddler for over 20 years, excelling both in whitewater kayak and canoeing. Noted for her acute eye in water reading, she excels in many environments from river running, creeking to slalom racing and finding the most efficient lines.

Teaching others how to do this effortlessly is a passion. She has been Kayak Chief Instructor for Whitewater Ontario and is an Advanced Instructor for Canoeing for Paddle Canada. She has also garnered many podium finishes Nationally and in North America in canoe slalom races.

Dave Woolridge - (British Columbia) Canoe Coach

Dave is a Master Canoe Instructor, Canada's Lead Big Canoe Instructor Trainer, a White Water Canoe Instructor, a Tripping Canoe Instructor and a Swift Water Rescue Technician.He has been an avid paddler since age 11> He's taught and led paddling trips all over Canada and the northwestern United States.

He's the Big Canoe Course Development Committee Leader for Paddle Canada andExecutive Board Member of the Recreational Canoe Association of BC. The man knows his boat; we're happy to have him with us again this year.

Dave Adler (Seakayak Coach)

Dave has been paddling since he was 8. When he was in high school in Washington D.C., he began training with the U.S. National Team in whitewater slalom kayaking, and earned a spot on the team in 1990. In winter months, he often drove to school with a helmet on, because it was frozen to his head.

By the time he was 19, he was the Head Instructor at the Madawaska Kanu Centre in Ontario. In 2001 he founded East Coast Outfitters- a paddling outfitter and school near Halifax, Nova Scotia. Dave is a Paddle Canada Instructor Trainer in sea kayaking, whitewater canoeing, and stand up paddle-boarding. He is a Master Learning Facilitator in Whitewater Kayaking with Canoe Kayak Canada.

His favourite boat is a dugout canoe that is sitting on a beach somewhere in Panama. At least that’s where he left it

Richard Alexander - Newfoundland
(St. John's Newfoundland and Labrador) Sea Kayak Coach

Richard is Past President of Paddle Canada. He is a level 4 Instructor and Instructor Trainer in the Sea Kayak program, holds multiple instructor designations in the canoe program and is the author of Paddle Canada’s Coastal Canoe Program.

A graduate of the College of the North Atlantic’s Outdoor Recreation program, Richard has many years of wilderness tripping under his belt including a sea kayak expedition up the west coast of Greenland that finished 400 miles north of the Arctic Circle.

For the last decade Richard has had the pleasure of exposing hundreds of people to the joys of paddling as the owner and course director of the Newfoundland Kayak Company.

Blair Doyle - (Dartmouth, NS) Paddle Canada President

Blair has been paddling in the Maritimes since he was introduced to a canoe in 1985. With Paddle Canada he is an advanced Lake Tandem and Solo Lake Canoe Instructor, Sea Kayak Level 2 Instructor and a River Kayak Level 1 Instructor.

Through teaching wilderness medical courses and being involved in Search and Rescue since 1988 he has become a strong supporter of certification and training. He is currently the President of Canoe Kayak Nova Scotia and President of Paddle Canada.

Jacqueline Holmes Sea Kayak Coach (Cape Breton)

Jacqueline Holmes grew up canoeing London, Ontario's Thames River with her father and has paddled in dugout canoes in South America and Haida Gwaii. She has led sea kayaking trips and instruction in BC, Baja and Atlantic Canada and has hung out with dolphins and manatees by stand-up paddleboard. Paddle with Jacqueline and you'll talk, laugh and smile your way through anything.

Amanda Harrington (Saint John) Seakayak and Canoe Coach

Amanda a graduate of the New Brunswick Community College - St. Andrews' Adventure Leadership program, and is currently finishing up her Bachelor of Recreation & Sports Studies at UNB Fredericton.

Amanda serves the Executive of CanoeKayakNB , and is a Paddle Canada Lake Canoe Instructor, Canoe Tripping Instructor, Stand-Up Paddleboard Instructor and Sea Kayak Instructor. She is also a Canoe Kayak Canada Level 2 Whitewater Kayak Instructor/Leader.

Tim Humes (New Brunswick) Canoe Coach

Tim Humes began paddling (badly, by his own admission) in 1972 on the Miramichi. Since then he has established his outdoor retail business-Eddy Out Depot- while taking Paddle Canada/Canoe Kayak New Brunswick courses to qualify him as a FW Instructor , LW Instructor, MW Instructor, Sea Kayak 1 Instructor and Poling Instructor.

Since his retirement from social work in '05, he has taken an even more active role in promoting safe recreational paddling through schools and clubs. He is served several executive roles with CKNB, presently as Past-President of CKNB. He is co-chair of the CKNB Heritage Committee and a prime mover behind the restoration of some ancient native portage trails in New Brunswick.

Graham Ketcheson- Paddle Canada Executive Director
(Kingston, ON) Sea Kayak Coach

Graham Ketcheson is currently working full time as Executive Director for Paddle Canada. Graham has worked for over 22 years in the outdoor and paddlesports industry as a Program / Marketing Consultant for 1000 Island Kayaking, a General Manager for White Squall Paddling Centre, and as a Canoe/Kayak Guide/Instructor for some of Canada’s leading outfitters and paddling schools from Newfoundland, to Nunavut to Vancouver Island.

Graham's passion for canoeing and kayaking stretch back before his professional paddlesport career. At the Muskoka, Ontario cottage he learned to solo canoe at 7 yrs old, then in his teens went on long canoe trips, with YMCA camps in northern Canada. Graham’s first guiding/instructor job was leading voyageur canoe trips down the Nahanni. Graham is a PC Level 3 Sea Kayak Instructor and Level 2 Instructor Trainer.

Rob Lemmon - (Fredericton, NB) White Water Kayak and Sea Kayak coach

Rob has been paddling for most his life, and teaching for most of adult life. He holds IT/Instructor certifications in all disciplines of the Paddle Canada program (Sea Kayak/Canoe/Whitewater/SUP) and serves on the Paddle Canada Program Development Committees for River Kayak and Canoeing and is the PC Board member for New Brunswick, and on the CanoeKayakNB Executive.

Rob teaches swiftwater rescue courses for Rescue Canada and owns and operates Maritime DayTripping.

Rory Matchett - (Moncton, NB) White Water Coach

Rory has been paddling, poling and "playing in canoes" for over 25 years.

He is a Canadian Recreational Canoe Association Moving Water Instructor, a Canoe New Brunswick Poling Instructor, a Boy Scout Leader and Trainer, and Treasurer for Canoe New Brunswick.

Kevin Silliker - (Sunny Corner, NB) Canoe Poling Coach

Kevin has been "messing with canoes" for over 30 years.

He is a Canadian Recreational Canoe Association Moving Water Instructor, a Canoe New Brunswick Poling Instructor, a Training Officer for New Brunswick Ground Search and Rescue, the Founder/Trainer of the Miramichi Search and Rescue River Team, Secretary of Canoe New Brunswick, and a Class I Guide.

Alan Stanley- (Charlottetown, PEI) Sea Kayak Coach

Alan has been an avid sea kayaker, an experienced wooden kayak builder, and an active Paddle Canada instructor for over a decade on Prince Edward Island.

A certified Level 3 paddler and a level 2 instructor, Alan has paddled extensively throughout the Atlantic provinces, and has introduced dozens of kayakers to their sport.

He was the PEI representative and the treasurer of Paddle Canada from 2008 to 2010.

Tim White Sea Kayak Coach (Cape Breton)

Tim White started paddling canoes in Boy Scouts by the age of 10, discovered sea kayaking at 22 and stand-up paddleboarding at 36. He has led expeditions and taught sea kayakers in Atlantic Canada, BC and Mexico since 1995. Ask him about paddling with alligators - it's the only thing on the water that has ever made him think twice.
